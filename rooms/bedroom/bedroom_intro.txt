You wake with a sharp, pulsing ache behind your forehead. The cold in the room sinks into your bones before you can fully sit up. Beneath you is a wooden bedframe—old, damp, and so warped that it groans under even the slightest movement.

A weak flashlight lies in your hand, trembling faintly when you raise it. Its thin beam reveals a suffocatingly small bedroom: peeling wallpaper hanging like dead skin, a wardrobe leaning in the corner, and dust drifting from the ceiling in soft gray sheets.

Two closed doors wait in the darkness:
Door A to your left.
Door B to your right.

The room feels abandoned, but not empty. The air is too still—too aware.
