You kneel and lift the flashlight toward the shadow beneath the bed. Dust clouds explode upward as your hand sweeps blindly along the floorboards. Your fingers brush something soft—fabric swollen with moisture.

You pull it out: a Damp Diary, its cover peeling, pages stuck together with water damage. One page can still be read:

“It is watching me… The key… is under… the water…”

You can't tell whether the handwriting shook from fear—or from something else entirely.
